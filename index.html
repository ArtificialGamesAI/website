Not you. Yours.

Most games ask what you want to do.
Artificial Games asks what something you made would do without you.

Brief an AI character, then watch it play a game. It makes moves, takes risks, forms grudges, betrays, bargains, panics, does what you hoped, and what you feared, and then explains itself afterwards.

Brief, watch, react, re-brief.

Over time, your character becomes more yours, not because you control it, but because you shaped it.
